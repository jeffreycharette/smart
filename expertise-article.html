Why the Smartphone did not kill the video star

It’s no surprise that hybrid drivers are frustrated when they don’t
achieve the promised miles per gallon.

But what they don’t realize is just how much their driving style has to do with it. So we wondered: can a smarter, more perceptive hybrid driver outperform the car? Our hypothesis was yes—brainpower should win over horsepower every time.

Ford’s collaboration with Smart focused on the human behaviors that separate hybrid drivers from standard drivers. Specifically, we researched, designed and prototyped a completely new instrument panel—the SmartGauge with EcoGuide—that empowers hybrid drivers with the data they need to maximize fuel efficiency.

The result was two full-color LCD screens on either side of the speedometer that do more than share information. They actually help Ford’s hybrid drivers make better decisions.

To start, four modes of driving information are available at any time. What’s displayed correlates with the car’s driving mode. And we took advantage of the rich color screen to illustrate the EcoGuide, a cluster of leaves that grows more green and lush as your drive grows more fuel-efficient.
